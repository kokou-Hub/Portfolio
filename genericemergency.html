Emergency Room Visits: Key Insights & Findings

The Emergency Room Visits Dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of patient activity, waiting times, and satisfaction levels. Here are the key takeaways:

1️⃣ Patient Volume & Trends

  • A total of 140 patient visits were recorded, reflecting a 12% increase in 2020 from the previous month.
  • The busiest day was Friday at 11:00 AM, indicating peak congestion during late mornings.

2️⃣ Wait Times & Hospital Efficiency

  • The average wait time was 35.82 minutes, showing a 0.2% decrease compared to last month.
  • The Orthopedics and Neurology departments had the longest wait times, suggesting potential resource allocation challenges.

3️⃣ Patient Demographics & Satisfaction

  • Patient visits were almost evenly split between males (71) and females (69).
  • The highest satisfaction scores were reported by Pacific Islanders (6.25) and Two or More Races (6.21), while African American patients had the lowest average satisfaction score (4.38).
  • Satisfaction improved by 4.8% from last month, reflecting better patient experiences or hospital service improvements.

4️⃣ Time & Gender-Based Trends

  • Tuesdays and Wednesdays showed higher visit rates than other days, suggesting midweek peaks.
  • The heatmap of patient visits by time shows higher activity during daytime hours, with early mornings and late nights seeing fewer visits.

5️⃣ Actionable Recommendations

  • Optimize staffing during peak hours (especially Fridays at 11 AM) to reduce wait times and patient congestion.
  • Investigate dissatisfaction factors among African American patients to enhance overall patient experience.
  • Improve wait time management in high-traffic departments like Orthopedics and Neurology to boost efficiency.

This analysis provides key insights into patient flow, hospital efficiency, and satisfaction levels, helping hospital administrators make data-driven improvements to emergency room operations.
